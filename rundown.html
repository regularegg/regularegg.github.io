The Run Down Case Study

How can owners share a communal dog run effectively?

Overview

This is a personal passion project inspired by my friends at the dog run. We always complain about how hard it is to meet up, so I decided to explore that problem through design.

  • Role:

    Researcher, Designer

  • Duration:

    2 Months

  • Skill:

    UX Design, UX Research, Product Design

  • Tools:

    Adobe XD, Sketch, InVision

Problem

Apartment dogs need to socialize

Owning a dog in the city is difficult, so a communal dog run is a lifesaver for owners with energetic pups. However, owners must time their visits accurately to enjoy the full benefits of the run. Owners often end up waiting alone for other dogs to show up or must hustle away when a dog in heat shows up.

Goal

Help owners effectively schedule play time

This project aims to create a lightweight app that helps owners communicate information about their dog's personality and preferences to the building's dog-owning community as quickly as possible.

Research

Interviews

I wanted to target dog owners living in high rise buildings with a communal dog run, a common situation in NYC. I interviewed several dog owners in my building to gain a better sense of their different personalities and needs. I found that dogs and owners were mostly different combinations of a few distinct personality groups.
